Types of Coffee

Coffee is a delicious and popular beverage that is made from roasted coffee beans. There are many different types of coffee that vary in taste, strength, and preparation method. Here is a table that shows some of the most common types of coffee that you can find around the world:

Type Origin Description
Espresso Italy A strong and concentrated coffee that is brewed by forcing hot water through finely ground coffee beans. It is usually served in a small cup and can be enjoyed on its own or as a base for other coffee drinks.
Cappuccino Italy A creamy and frothy coffee that is made by mixing espresso with steamed milk and foam. It is usually topped with cinnamon or chocolate powder and can be served hot or cold.
Latte Italy A smooth and milky coffee that is made by adding steamed milk to espresso. It is usually served in a large glass or mug and can be flavored with syrups such as vanilla, caramel, or hazelnut.
Americano USA A simple and diluted coffee that is made by adding hot water to espresso. It is similar to regular black coffee but has a richer and stronger flavor.
Mocha Yemen A sweet and chocolatey coffee that is made by adding chocolate syrup or powder to espresso and steamed milk. It is usually served in a large mug and can be topped with whipped cream and chocolate shavings.
Macchiato Italy A bold and balanced coffee that is made by adding a small amount of steamed milk and foam to espresso. It is usually served in a small cup and can be marked with a dot of foam or chocolate syrup.
Flat White Australia/New Zealand A velvety and smooth coffee that is made by adding steamed milk to espresso. It is similar to a latte but has a higher ratio of coffee to milk and less foam.
Cold Brew USA A refreshing and smooth coffee that is made by steeping coarsely ground coffee beans in cold water for several hours. It is usually served over ice and can be sweetened with milk, sugar, or syrups.
